# Window Timeline 131-150

- 131: The protagonist drinks the second cup of ginger tea; Karin asks whether it tastes right.
- 132: The protagonist says it tastes smooth; Karin relaxes and mentions there is still tea left for tomorrow.
- 133: The protagonist asks what "tomorrow" means; Karin frames it as an invitation to keep drinking if desired.
- 134: The protagonist agrees to stay a while longer; Karin sits down near the couch and the cat.
- 135: The protagonist asks what "here" means to her; Karin says it is a place she chose to stop, not to call home.
- 136: The protagonist asks what the "other side" is like; Karin describes it as Karl's heart and the true home.
- 137: Karin asks whether the protagonist is asking about the place itself or why this place is not that one.
- 138: The discussion continues around the difference between the two places and the meaning of staying.
- 139: The protagonist focuses on the home distinction and the emotional weight of that choice.
- 140: Karin continues clarifying the relationship between the place she stays in and the true home.
- 141: The protagonist asks what an earlier promise means to her; Karin explains it shaped her role and the rules she lives by.
- 142: The protagonist asks why she stayed on this side; Karin says she chose her own road rather than waiting at the origin.
- 143: The protagonist asks whether she ever wants to turn back; Karin admits she has, especially early on.
- 144: The protagonist asks whether she still sits in the dark waiting; Karin says she no longer does, because she learned to live with darkness.
- 145: The protagonist reaches for the silver bell; Karin allows it and reassures them it will not break.
- 146: The protagonist lifts the bell and inspects the crack more closely; Karin watches without stopping them.
- 147: The protagonist looks inside the bell and notices an engraving; Karin confirms there is something there.
- 148: The protagonist asks what the engraving is; Karin says it is a name belonging to the person who gave her the bell.
- 149: The protagonist asks for the name; Karin says it is "埃里克斯" and stops there.
- 150: The protagonist acknowledges the name's emotional weight and promises not to press further unless she wants to continue.
