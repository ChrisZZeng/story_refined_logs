# Window Timeline 81-100

## Context Turns 81-90

- 81: Player asks Karl which entrance she stands at. Karl says she stands at the entrance of Dark Street and is "the street itself."
- 82: Player asks what Karl can see as Dark Street itself. Karl describes seeing currents, footprints, and subtle changes around Connor, Karina, and the player.
- 83: Player asks where the current points. Karl says it points toward an unseen person connecting Connor, port water levels, and Mint.
- 84: Player suggests returning. Karina and Karl leave the park and start walking back.
- 85: Player observes Karl and Karina walking. They arrive at a door; Karina opens it and Karl enters.
- 86: Player asks where they will stay tonight. Karina says not here, tells Karl to bring the silver bell, and leads the player toward the "real home."
- 87: Player reminds Karina about the bell. Karina shows the silver bell already in her hand and says Karl brought it out.
- 88: Player asks what the "real home" is like. Karina says it is an old warehouse, the past shadow of Dark Street, on the "other side."
- 89: Player asks what "other side" means. Karina says it is not geographical and can be reached with a word.
- 90: Player asks what "with a word" means. Karina explains that saying a place's name from the right position has weight, then walks toward a dark boundary.

## Evaluated Turns 91-100

- 91: Player looks at their shadow and steps forward. Karina says a short unknown word, crosses the boundary with Karl, and the player follows into darkness. Choices ask how to investigate the new place or the word.
- 92: Player looks around. The darkness gives way to a warm warehouse with tungsten bulbs, old floorboards, boxes, a long table, an open old book, an oil lamp, a fireplace, a rug, Karina, and Karl. Choices invite more detailed inspection or interaction.
- 93: Player inspects the warehouse more carefully. The text details lights, floor, iron boxes, the long table, cups, an open old book with its spine/back upward, the fireplace, Karl, and Karina. Choices include inspecting the old book.
- 94: Player goes to the long table to inspect the open old book. The book is again described as open with its spine upward, yet the player looks at page contents and turns pages with unfamiliar symbols.
- 95: Player asks what is written. Karina explains that she does not know all usages, says the book records space rather than ordinary information, then closes the book and pushes it toward the table center.
- 96: Player asks what the "other side" actually is. Karina explains it as home, Karl's anchored place, and the body/original of which Dark Street is the shadow.
- 97: Player asks what "feeling" the book means. The narration describes the player's gaze returning to the open old book and visible symbols, despite the book being closed in turn 95.
- 98: Player recalls the smell when turning pages. The text describes paper, plant-like ink, dry dust, and old smoke absorbed into the paper. Karina confirms this is part of feeling the book.
- 99: Player asks what Karina felt the first time she touched the book. Karina recalls feeling warmth from the book and explains how it records the surrounding space.
- 100: Player asks whether Karina later learned to feel the symbols. Karina explains slowly sensing warmer symbols and links between symbols and the warehouse's physical features.
