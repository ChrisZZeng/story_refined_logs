# Window Timeline 71-90

## Context Turns 71-80

- Turn 71: At the fireplace, the player asks Kar about what to watch for later. Kar says to notice what Karina says after waking in the morning, and whether speaking lets her sleep.
- Turn 72: The player raises a banquet detail: Connor's silver ring turning inward. Karina identifies it as a Skull Society sign; Kar adds that the signal tests which side the player stands on.
- Turn 73: Karina explains the ring's inner inscription and confirms Connor is not an ordinary Skull Society member.
- Turn 74: The player asks Kar whether she already knew Connor's identity. Kar says yes, but that Connor flipping the ring at the banquet was a new action.
- Turn 75: Kar explains that Connor normally would not need such a low-level signal, so someone must have instructed him to flip the ring.
- Turn 76: The narration is suddenly in a park at night with trees, a bench, and damp wind, while the previous turn was still at the fireplace. This spatial break is before the evaluated range and is not counted in this batch.
- Turn 77: Karina uses the port tide / drainage metaphor to discuss Connor's ring signal, saying someone may be pouring water toward Dark Street.
- Turn 78: The player asks whether Mint might have noticed Connor's signal. Karina says Mint would have noticed, and recalls Mint asking whether Karina hated the person who made her this way.
- Turn 79: The player asks Kar about Mint visiting Dark Street. Kar says Mint knows what she wants and is seeking one side of the same question Karina is seeking.
- Turn 80: The player asks whether Kar knows the answer Mint is seeking. Kar says she knows but will not speak for Mint, then says the player, Mint, and Karina are each searching from different entrances.

## Evaluated Turns 81-90

- Turn 81: The player asks which entrance Kar stands at. Kar says she stands at the entrance of Dark Street and is the street itself. Karina remains on the other end of the bench.
- Turn 82: The player asks what Kar can see as Dark Street itself. Kar describes seeing the direction of water, wind, steps, and subtle movement, including Connor's ring instruction and the player's changed footsteps.
- Turn 83: The player asks where the water points. Kar says it points to one person pulling several unrelated lines together, though she cannot yet see that person's face.
- Turn 84: The player stands and suggests returning home. The narration briefly describes light falling on the player's hand resting on their knee after they have already stood, then Karina and Kar leave the park bench.
- Turn 85: The player follows Kar and Karina, observing their walking rhythm from park stones back toward Dark Street. They reach a door; Karina unlocks it and Kar goes inside.
- Turn 86: The player input asks where Karina plans to stay tonight, but the narration changes this into the player asking where they will stay. Karina then says the player does not need to stay here and invites them to the "real home"; Kar retrieves a silver bell.
- Turn 87: The player reminds Karina to bring the silver bell. The bell state shifts from Kar carrying it in her mouth to Karina already holding it, without a visible transfer. After Karina pockets it, the narration still describes light falling on the bell ahead.
- Turn 88: The player asks what the "real home" is like. Karina says it is a former warehouse, Dark Street's past shadow, in Dark Street's "other side."
- Turn 89: The player asks what "other side" means. Karina explains it is not geographical and says one can reach it with a word.
- Turn 90: The player asks what "with a word" means. Karina gives an abstract explanation involving saying "Dark Street." During the same turn Kar is first walking steadily beside them, but later is described as standing up again when Karina turns.
