# Window Timeline 21-40

Only turns 31-40 were counted for this batch. Turns 21-30 were used as context.

## Context: Turns 21-30

- 21-22: Paz softly says Minte's name. The scene repeats the same recognition beat: Minte freezes, adopts a defensive posture, then half-forms Paz's name.
- 23-25: Paz waits, asks why she is here, and explains he came because an anonymous photo showed her alive in New Sicily.
- 26-27: Minte says she has a line to investigate in New Sicily and asks what the anonymous letter contained. Paz says it only had time, place, and her photo.
- 28-29: Minte names the Skull Society as the line she is tracking, calls it tied to things behind the war, and refuses to say more.
- 30: Paz stops pressing the Skull Society topic and asks how she has been all these years.

## Evaluated: Turns 31-40

- 31: Paz waits. Minte answers only that she is alive, not dead, and "that's all."
- 32: Paz pushes gently on the insufficiency of "alive"; Minte asks what he wants to hear and says she has nothing to say.
- 33: Paz waits quietly. The turn says Minte opens her mouth, but no speech follows; choices still ask the player to wait for her to open up.
- 34: Minte actually begins explaining: after the Middle East, someone pulled her from the ruins; she survived alone and says she thought nobody would come, because she does not deserve it.
- 35: Paz says he came because she did not die. Minte repeats "you came" and moves a little closer emotionally and physically.
- 36: Paz asks what she means by not deserving it. Minte says he and the others should have lived instead.
- 37: Paz silently reaches out and places his palm over her hand, but this conflicts with the preceding visible hand pose.
- 38: Paz keeps his hand there. Minte gradually relaxes and curls her hand under his palm.
- 39: Paz lifts his hand away and steps back half a step, making space while staying present.
- 40: Paz continues waiting. The text largely repeats the silence and small physical gestures without new information.
