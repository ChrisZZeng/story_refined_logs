# Window Timeline 11-30

## Context Turns 11-20

- Turn 11: The player pushes open the apparent tailor-shop door and sees a worktable, fabric, an old treadle sewing machine, and no visible person.
- Turn 12: The player asks if anyone is there. An elderly woman answers from inside, tells the player to enter and close the door, and identifies herself as the “tailor”.
- Turn 13: The player says they can pay and asks the price. The tailor refuses money and asks the player to retrieve an item from deep in Dark Street.
- Turn 14: The player asks for another way. The tailor says there is none, except returning to search the antique-street cat nest.
- Turn 15: The player asks what the item is and why the tailor cannot go. The tailor says it is in a locked iron box under a loose floorboard in the rear room of an abandoned butcher shop.
- Turn 16: The player asks about guards and precautions. The tailor explains that the butcher-shop area is safer by day, black-gang people may appear near dusk, and the rear-room lock is weak.
- Turn 17: The player chooses to wait until dusk and use the festival crowd. The tailor gives timing advice and says the door will be left for the player.
- Turn 18: The player hides near the tailor shop to wait for dusk.
- Turn 19: The player waits until the drum band and festival crowd approach Dark Street at dusk, then joins the flow.
- Turn 20: The player follows the crowd, observes gang-like men, territorial marks, and the cracked butcher-shop sign ahead, then chooses a side alley route.

## Evaluated Turns 21-30

- Turn 21: The player takes a narrow side alley and arrives near the butcher shop sign.
- Turn 22: The player checks the side of the building and finds a rear door, a boarded window, fresh signs near the threshold, and no immediate sound behind the door.
- Turn 23: The player returns to the front and observes the butcher shop from behind an oil drum; the front door is closed and no guard is visible.
- Turn 24: The player retreats toward the main alley and hides behind stacked boxes; a broad-shouldered man in a dark jacket appears near the butcher-shop front area and leaves toward Antique Street.
- Turn 25: The player follows the man at a distance into a narrower alley and sees him continue under a weak hanging bulb.
- Turn 26: The player approaches the sound source and sees the man handling metal objects near an overturned iron cage and an open canvas bag.
- Turn 27: The player stops following and returns to the main alley; black-jacketed men confront the player, then Karina intervenes and invites the player to a place where they can talk.
- Turn 28: The player asks where Karina is taking them; she answers “apartment”, and the narration makes the player follow her into deeper Dark Street.
- Turn 29: The player follows Karina while memorizing landmarks and reaches a building with a half-boarded facade and a stairwell beyond the door.
- Turn 30: The player asks whether the building is safe; Karina says Dark Street has no absolute safety, but her place follows rules and is safer than outside if the player follows rules too.
